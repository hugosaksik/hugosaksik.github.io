La simplicité et l'écran

Master Thesis

La simplicité et l'écran is a master thesis redacted during my master degree at the ESADSE. It draws the original intentions that motivated the conception of the first digital graphical user interfaces, and question how simplicity became the required paradigm of contemporary user interface design. Based on theoretician's writings such as Lev Manovich, Matthew Fuller, and Anthony Masure, a criticism is built upon the non-desirable effects of this dominant design philosophy.

With the desire to join the ideas I develop in this writing and my design practice, the layout of this thesis was conceived with html, css and javascript ; with the precious help of the paged.js library. The source code of the layout is open and released under the AGPL-3.0 License on my github account.

The front-page pattern suggesting a terminal of an old phosphor monitor was made with processing. The pattern being generated in a procedural way, each printed book displays a unique laser-cut front page.
